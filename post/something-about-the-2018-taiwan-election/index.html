Something About The 2018 Taiwan Election

Election Taiwan

It is the first time that the mayor election combined with referendum in 2018/11/24.

Because there are many referndum issues, if voters hadn’t prepared enough before going to vote, they would have spent much time to realize the descriptions of the referendum issues.

Before arriving the reception desk, I’d been lined up for 1 and half hours. I almost complete one third of the novel ‘Mist Born’ in my kobo ereader. After I casted the vote, I saw my mom in the middle of the line. She is not in good health, so I let her sit in the park and lined up for her.

After 4:00 pm, many polling station had been started counting the ballots. But there are still many people hadn’t casted their vote. It caused that people who hadn’t completed the voting process can see the current number of votes of each candidates. Finally we spent two and half hours to complete the whole voting process. What a terrible voting experience!

Even though the voting process took a lot of time, the result is not as my wish. Almost every candidates and referendums I voted lost the election. But I think I can realize and accept the result. In democratic country, everyone can express their opinions through voting, but most people do not spend enough time to care and realize the candidates platform and referendum issues. If you randomly find someone on the street and ask how they evaluate a candidate, I thinks there’s seldom people can give a rational reason, especially old man(I think I have age discrimination XD…). Most of them just vote by their ‘feeling’.

But maybe we should not be that pessimistic. Ireland had been a country where abortion is illegal. After 16 years and 6 times referendum, they finally abolish the legislation of preventing abortion. They also pass same-sex marriage in 2015. I think Taiwan can refer to their experience and do more preparation for next referendum.
